# Phase 5 Research: Go-to-Market

**Phase:** 05 — Go-to-Market
**Researched:** 2026-03-20
**Overall confidence:** HIGH
**Requirements:** GTM-01, GTM-02, GTM-03, GTM-04, GTM-05

---

## Executive Summary

Phase 5 is a sales and marketing execution phase, not a code phase. It runs in Wave A (parallel with website rebrand and compliance) because the goal is to pre-sell founding members while the product is being built. The five requirements break into three workstreams: (1) personal brand positioning (GTM-01: LinkedIn), (2) demo asset creation (GTM-02: video), and (3) prospecting and closing (GTM-03/04/05: target list, outreach, signatures). This phase is the most critical in the entire roadmap because revenue = survival for a solo founder with zero runway.

The research shows the Dutch market is wide open for this positioning. There are zero dedicated AI agent platforms for Dutch marketing agencies. The 95%-adopted-but-5%-value-extracted gap means agencies are primed to buy a "done for you" agent rather than another tool. LinkedIn is the dominant channel for reaching Dutch agency owners, and a 5-touch outreach sequence with value-first engagement is the proven approach for B2B SaaS at this price point.

The founding member model is well-validated for this exact scenario: real product not fully proven, genuine capacity scarcity (solo founder), and early users who get real advantages (locked pricing, feature influence). The funnel math is realistic: 50 targets, 15 DM responses, 8 calls, 3-5 closes.

## Key Findings

### GTM-01: LinkedIn Profile Optimization

**Confidence: HIGH** (multiple current sources agree)

The LinkedIn profile must function as a landing page, not a resume. In 2026, LinkedIn's algorithm and AI search weigh the headline most heavily for discoverability. The profile must be structured for both human readers and AI agents that recommend experts.

**Headline formula:** "I help [target audience] achieve [specific outcome] through [specialty]"

- Recommended: "Ik help marketingbureaus opschalen zonder personeel | AI Marketing Medewerker | Oprichter FutureMarketingAI"
- English variant: "I help marketing agencies scale without hiring | AI Marketing Employee | Founder FutureMarketingAI"

**Profile elements that matter:**

1. **Headline** (most indexed field) -- outcome-focused, not title-focused
2. **Banner image** -- visual reinforcing "AI Marketing Employee for agencies" with product screenshot or value prop
3. **About section** -- structured for machine readability: problem, solution, proof, CTA
4. **Featured section** -- founding member landing page link, demo video (once created), case study from SKC
5. **Experience** -- FutureMarketingAI as current role with description matching headline narrative
6. **Digital footprint alignment** -- posts, comments, and profile must tell the same story or AI deems you low-authority

**Algorithm insight (2026):** Comments carry 15x more algorithmic weight than likes. The 3-Touch Rule before connecting: (1) comment on prospect's post, (2) reply to their comment elsewhere, (3) send connection request. This lifts response rates by 32%.

**Content cadence:** 3-5 posts per week. Highest engagement types for SaaS founders:

- Personal stories with business lessons (1.5-2x engagement)
- Original data/research (1.3-1.5x)
- Contrarian industry takes (1.2-1.5x)
- Document carousels (1.3-1.8x)

**Language strategy:** Mix Dutch and English. Dutch for NL agency owners (trust + cultural fit), English for broader reach and UK targeting.

### GTM-02: Demo Video

**Confidence: HIGH** (multiple current sources agree)

The demo video must be under 5 minutes per the requirement, but research strongly suggests 2-3 minutes is the sweet spot for self-served demos. Viewers tune out of passive video content quickly.

**Structure recommendation (3-minute target):**

1. **Hook (0:00-0:15):** Address the pain -- "Your agency uses 15 tools but still can't scale. What if you had one AI employee that runs them all?"
2. **Problem (0:15-0:45):** Three pain points: tool fragmentation, hiring bottleneck, reporting overhead
3. **Solution walkthrough (0:45-2:15):** Show 2-3 agent skills in action:
   - Content Creator skill: show R&P pipeline producing branded content
   - Social Media Manager: show scheduled posts going out across platforms
   - Reporting: show automated client report generation
4. **Results (2:15-2:45):** "38 hours saved per week" / "Scale from 10 to 50 clients" / cost anchor vs salary
5. **CTA (2:45-3:00):** "10 founding member spots. EUR 997/mo. All skills included."

**Production approach:** Screen recording with voiceover is sufficient for a founding member demo. Loom or similar tool. No need for expensive production -- authenticity matters more for early-stage B2B SaaS. Use the actual dashboard and n8n workflows (even if showing SKC data as a real case study).

**Key principle:** Show outcomes, not features. The viewer asks "Will this solve my problem?" not "What buttons does it have?"

**Distribution:** LinkedIn native video (better reach than YouTube links), website founding member page, DM attachment in outreach sequence.

### GTM-03: Target Agency Identification (50 agencies)

**Confidence: HIGH** (approach well-documented)

**Tool: LinkedIn Sales Navigator** (Core plan, ~EUR 80/mo)

Sales Navigator is the right tool because:

- Geography filter: Netherlands (country) + specific cities (Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, Den Haag, Eindhoven)
- Industry filter: Marketing Services, Advertising, Digital Marketing
- Company size: 2-50 employees (maps to 3-12 FTE target)
- You can save lead lists and get alerts on activity

**Building the NL list (30 agencies):**

1. Sales Navigator search: Industry = "Marketing Services" OR "Advertising" + Geography = "Netherlands" + Company Headcount = "11-50" or "2-10"
2. Filter for companies with recent posts (signal of active digital presence)
3. Identify the founder/owner/managing director (decision maker)
4. Qualify against ICP: performance/growth focus, not branding/creative
5. Check their client portfolio (visible on website) -- do they serve multiple clients? That means they need to scale

**ICP qualification checklist per agency:**

- [ ] 3-12 employees (check LinkedIn company page)
- [ ] Performance/growth/content focus (check website services)
- [ ] Founder is active on LinkedIn (check recent posts)
- [ ] Serves multiple clients (check portfolio/case studies)
- [ ] Not a creative/branding-only agency (anti-ICP)
- [ ] Based in NL or UK

**Building the UK list (20 agencies):**
Same approach but Geography = "United Kingdom", focus on London and Manchester. Target agencies comfortable with EUR pricing (or offer GBP equivalent).

**Tracking:** Simple spreadsheet or Notion database with columns: Agency Name, Contact, LinkedIn URL, Website, Size, Type, ICP Score, Outreach Stage, Notes, Last Touch Date.

**Alternative prospecting sources:**

- DDMA member directory (360+ organizations)
- Emerce 100 list (top Dutch digital companies)
- Clutch.co / Sortlist agency directories filtered by NL
- Google "marketing bureau [city]" for smaller agencies not on LinkedIn

### GTM-04: Outreach Sequence

**Confidence: HIGH** (B2B outreach methodology well-documented)

The outreach sequence follows a value-first, multi-touch approach. LinkedIn outreach gets 10-25% response rates vs 1-5% for cold email. For a EUR 997/mo commitment, expect a 3-14 day decision cycle from engaged prospects.

**Recommended 5-touch LinkedIn sequence:**

**Touch 1 (Day 0): Warm engagement**

- Find and comment on the agency owner's recent LinkedIn post
- Comment must be substantive (not "Great post!") -- add insight, share experience, ask a thoughtful question
- Goal: Get on their radar, no pitch

**Touch 2 (Day 2-3): Connection request**

- Short, personalized connection request
- Template (Dutch): "Hoi [naam], ik zag je post over [onderwerp] -- herkenbaar. Ik bouw een AI marketing medewerker specifiek voor bureaus zoals [bureau naam]. Leuk om te connecten!"
- Template (English): "Hi [name], saw your post about [topic] -- resonated with me. Building an AI marketing employee specifically for agencies like [agency]. Would be great to connect."
- No pitch in the connection request

**Touch 3 (Day 5-7): Value message after connection accepted**

- Share a relevant insight or piece of content (not a pitch)
- Template: "Bedankt voor de connectie! Ik deel dit even -- [link to relevant LinkedIn post about AI for agencies / GaaS trend]. Benieuwd hoe jullie bij [bureau] met AI bezig zijn?"
- Goal: Start a conversation

**Touch 4 (Day 10-14): The soft pitch**

- Reference the conversation or their content
- Template: "Ik ben nu 10 founding member plekken aan het vullen voor bureaus die als eerste een AI marketing medewerker willen inzetten. Alles inbegrepen voor EUR 997/mo -- normaal EUR 1.997. Zou je een korte demo willen zien? 15 min, geen verplichtingen."
- Attach or link the demo video

**Touch 5 (Day 18-21): Follow-up / closing the loop**

- If no response: "Hoi [naam], snap dat het druk is. Nog 6 van de 10 plekken beschikbaar. Mocht het timing zijn -- ik houd een plek open tot [datum]. Laat het me weten!"
- If responded but not committed: Address specific objection, offer a call

**Key principles:**

- Personalize every message (reference their agency, their posts, their clients)
- Never pitch in the first two touches
- Use urgency honestly ("6 of 10 spots left" -- only if true)
- Mix Dutch and English based on the prospect's LinkedIn language
- Track everything in the spreadsheet

**Volume:** 20-30 new outreach touches per week. With 50 targets, this takes 2-3 weeks to reach everyone.

### GTM-05: Closing Founding Members (3-5 signatures)

**Confidence: MEDIUM** (approach proven, but closing depends on execution and market response)

**The call structure (15-30 minutes):**

1. **Discovery (5 min):** "Hoeveel klanten bedienen jullie? Wat is jullie grootste bottleneck om te groeien? Welke tools gebruiken jullie?"
2. **Pain amplification (3 min):** "Wat kost het jullie als je een klant moet weigeren? Hoeveel uur per week gaat naar reporting?"
3. **Demo (10 min):** Walk through the 2-3 most relevant skills for their pain
4. **Founding member pitch (5 min):** "10 plekken, EUR 997/mo, alle skills, 6 maanden. Directe lijn met mij. Je betaalt minder dan de helft van wat een junior kost."
5. **Objection handling (5 min):** See objection map below
6. **Close:** "Zal ik de overeenkomst opsturen? Je kunt binnen 14 dagen cancelen als het niks is."

**Objection handling map:**

| Objection                                       | Response                                                                                             |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "EUR 997/mo is veel"                            | "Een junior kost EUR 3-5K/mo. Dit is een derde daarvan, werkt 24/7, wordt niet ziek."                |
| "AI kwaliteit is niet goed genoeg"              | "Laat me het je laten zien met jouw klantdata. 2-weken pilot, geen risico."                          |
| "Ik heb geen tijd om weer iets nieuws te leren" | "Wij onboarden alles. Jij hoeft niks te configureren -- founding members krijgen white-glove setup." |
| "Wat als ik stop?"                              | "6 maanden commitment, daarna maandelijks opzegbaar. Je data is van jou."                            |
| "Ik wil eerst resultaten zien"                  | "Fair. Founding members krijgen 30 dagen om waarde te ervaren. Niet tevreden? We praten."            |

**Founding member agreement elements:**

- EUR 997/mo, 6-month minimum commitment
- All skills included (normally EUR 1,997/mo Growth tier)
- Price locked for life while subscription active
- Direct founder access (WhatsApp/Slack channel)
- Monthly strategy call
- Feature request priority
- Must provide feedback and testimonial rights

**Expected conversion funnel:**

```
50 target agencies
 -> 35 connection requests accepted (70%)
 -> 15 DM conversations started (43% of accepted)
 -> 8 demo calls booked (53% of conversations)
 -> 3-5 founding members signed (38-63% of calls)
```

This funnel is realistic based on:

- LinkedIn connection acceptance: 50-70% for personalized requests
- DM response: 30-50% after warm engagement
- Demo to close: 15-25% for founding member offers (higher than normal due to exclusivity + discount)

## Critical Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Pitching Too Early

**What goes wrong:** Sending sales messages before building rapport. Prospects ignore or block.
**Prevention:** Enforce the 3-touch warm-up rule. No pitch in first two touches. Track engagement status in spreadsheet.

### Pitfall 2: Generic Outreach

**What goes wrong:** Copy-paste messages that feel automated. Response rates drop to 1-3%.
**Prevention:** Every message must reference something specific about the agency (their recent post, their client, their team size). Limit to 10-15 new outreaches per day to maintain quality.

### Pitfall 3: Demo Video as Feature Tour

**What goes wrong:** 5-minute video showing every button and menu. Viewer drops off at 30 seconds.
**Prevention:** Start with pain, show 2-3 outcomes, end with CTA. No more than 3 minutes. Test with 3 people before publishing.

### Pitfall 4: No Urgency Mechanism

**What goes wrong:** Prospects say "interesting, let me think about it" and never return.
**Prevention:** Real scarcity (10 spots, show remaining count). Deadline ("founding window closes June 30 or when spots fill"). Public updates on LinkedIn ("3 spots left").

### Pitfall 5: Neglecting Dutch Cultural Directness

**What goes wrong:** Using American-style hype ("revolutionary", "game-changing") alienates Dutch buyers.
**Prevention:** Dutch buyers want: "Wat doet het? Wat kost het? Bewijs het." Lead with data, ROI math, and practical results. No superlatives.

### Pitfall 6: Trying to Close in DMs

**What goes wrong:** Complex B2B sale at EUR 997/mo cannot close in text messages alone.
**Prevention:** DMs are for booking calls only. Always push to a 15-minute video call for the actual pitch and close.

### Pitfall 7: Not Tracking Outreach State

**What goes wrong:** Sending duplicate messages, forgetting to follow up, losing track of who is where in the funnel.
**Prevention:** Simple spreadsheet with columns: Name, Company, LinkedIn, Stage (warm/connected/DM'd/call-booked/call-done/pitched/signed/lost), Last Touch, Next Action, Notes.

## Research Gaps and Open Questions

### Resolved with HIGH confidence:

- LinkedIn profile optimization approach
- Outreach sequence structure
- Demo video format and length
- Target identification methodology
- Founding member offer structure

### Needs validation during execution:

- **Actual response rates in NL market** -- benchmarks are global averages, NL may differ (LOW confidence)
- **Whether 50 targets is enough** -- funnel math assumes optimistic conversion; may need 75-100 targets if rates are lower
- **Pricing objection frequency** -- EUR 997/mo is validated by research but actual NL agency response is unknown
- **Demo video script effectiveness** -- will need A/B testing of hooks
- **UK outreach effectiveness** -- cross-border selling from NL to UK adds friction (timezone, currency, trust)

### Not researched (out of scope for this phase):

- Content marketing strategy (SEO, blog) -- this is Month 3+ activity
- Partnership development (DDMA, HubSpot) -- this is Month 3+ activity
- Paid advertising -- premature before messaging is validated
- Email outreach tooling -- LinkedIn is primary channel
