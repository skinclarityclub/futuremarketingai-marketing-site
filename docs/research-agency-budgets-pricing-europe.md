# Marketing Agency Budgets & Pricing Research: Netherlands & UK

Research conducted to understand what marketing agencies SPEND on tools, what they CHARGE clients, and what they'd pay for AI platforms.

---

## 1. What Agencies Charge Their Clients

### Netherlands (2026 data)

| Service | Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| SEO | EUR 800 - 2,000/mo |
| Google Ads (SEA) management | EUR 500 - 1,500/mo + ad spend |
| Social media advertising | EUR 500 - 1,500/mo + ad spend |
| Content marketing | EUR 600 - 2,000/mo |
| Email marketing | EUR 400 - 1,200/mo |
| **Full-service package** | **EUR 2,000 - 5,000/mo** |

**Retainer ranges (NL):**
- Small bureaus: EUR 800/mo
- MKB (SME) typical: EUR 1,000 - 2,500/mo
- Larger full-service: EUR 5,000+/mo
- Hourly rates: EUR 75 - 175/hr (junior EUR 75-90, senior/specialist EUR 100-150)

**Source:** SearchLab.nl (2026), Trustoo.nl, eRocket.nl

**Key NL insight:** AI is already disrupting pricing. Bureaus using AI effectively offer **30-50% lower rates** at comparable quality. A retainer that cost EUR 2,500/mo in 2023 now goes for EUR 1,500/mo.

## 2. Agency Revenue, Size & Retainer Distribution

### Global agency retainer survey (SparkToro/Benchpress 2025/2026)

**85% of agencies work on retainer basis** (up from 81% in 2024).

**Monthly retainer distribution:**
- 49% of agencies: $1,000 - $5,000/mo average retainer
- 31% of agencies: $5,000 - $10,000/mo
- 6% of agencies: $10,000 - $25,000/mo
- 10% of agencies: $25,000 - $50,000/mo
- **86% total have retainers of $10k or less**

**By agency size (Predictable Profits 2025, 300+ agencies):**

| Agency Size | Pricing Model | Avg Monthly Retainer | Typical Client Count |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small (10-24 FTE) | Hourly/Project | Under $5,000 | 18 clients |
| Medium (25-49 FTE) | Mixed | $5,000 - $10,000 | 28 clients |
| Large (50+ FTE) | Value-Based | $10,000+ | 48 clients |

### Revenue benchmarks

|---|---|---|---|
| 7-figure | ~10-25 | $1M - $2M | $150,000 |
| 8-figure | 50+ | $10M+ | $225,000 |

**Client lifetime value:**
- 7-figure agencies: $125,000 over 18 months
- 8-figure agencies: $450,000 over 36 months

**Source:** Predictable Profits Agency Growth 2025 report (300+ agencies)

## 3. Agency Profit Margins

**Industry average net profit margin: 6-12%** (mycodelesswebsite.com, industry benchmarks)

**By performance tier:**
- Average/struggling agencies: 6-12% net margin
- 7-figure agencies: 18-22% profit margin
- 8-figure agencies: 25-32% profit margin
- Top 3% of agencies: 43% profit margins
- Niche/specialized agencies: 40-75% gross margins
- Lean agency with strong positioning: 20-40% net margins achievable

**Source:** Predictable Profits, LinkedIn industry analysis

**Key insight:** The spread is enormous. Most agencies operate on thin margins (6-12%), while niche specialists can hit 40-75% gross margins through specialization and AI adoption.

## 4. Agency Tool/Software Spend

### SaaS spend per employee (all industries)

**$4,830 per employee per year** (21.9% increase YoY)

- Average organization wastes $21M annually on unused SaaS licenses
- Average company uses 106 SaaS apps
- 7% of apps are AI-enabled (growing fast)
- AI-native app spending surged 75.2% YoY
- 57% of SaaS buyers anticipate spending more in the next year

**Source:** Zylo 2025 SaaS Management Index

### Agency-specific tool spend

**Key finding from Basis 2025 Advertising Agency Report:**
- Number of agencies juggling **10+ tools jumped 131%** in 12 months
- Inefficient processes: #1 challenge for agencies (56%)
- Rising costs: #2 challenge (43%)
- Shrinking profits: #3 challenge (43%)
- "Siloed/disconnected systems" = #2 biggest challenge (45%)

**Typical agency tool stack costs (estimated from multiple sources):**
- Small agency (5-10 people): EUR/GBP 500-2,000/mo in tools
- Mid-size agency (15-30 people): EUR/GBP 2,000-5,000/mo in tools
- Large agency (50+ people): EUR/GBP 5,000-15,000/mo in tools

This translates to roughly **EUR 100-500 per employee per month** on tools, or **3-8% of revenue** for most agencies.

## 8. Pricing Ceiling Analysis for FMai AaaS Platform

### The math: What would an agency pay for AI tools?

**Scenario: Typical NL/UK small-mid agency**
- 10-20 clients
- EUR/GBP 2,000-5,000/mo per client retainer
- Monthly revenue: EUR/GBP 30,000-80,000
- Net margin: 18-22% (7-figure agency)
- Net profit: EUR/GBP 5,400-17,600/mo
- Current tool spend: EUR/GBP 1,000-3,000/mo (estimated 3-8% of revenue)

### Willingness to pay thresholds

**Conservative (replace tools):** EUR 300-800/mo
- Replaces existing tool stack partially
- Must save time/money vs current setup
- "Another SaaS tool" positioning

**Mid-range (augment capacity):** EUR 800-2,000/mo
- Saves 1-2 FTE worth of work
- AI handles content, reporting, campaign optimization
- Equivalent to hiring a part-time junior

**Aggressive (replace headcount):** EUR 2,000-5,000/mo
- Demonstrably replaces 1+ full employee
- At EUR 3,000/mo = EUR 36,000/yr vs EUR 40,000-60,000/yr employee
- 25-40% savings vs headcount
- This is where the REAL value proposition lives

### Ceiling calculation

If an agency charges EUR 3,000-5,000/mo per client and has 10 clients:
- Monthly revenue: EUR 30,000-50,000
- At 5% of revenue allocated to AI tools: EUR 1,500-2,500/mo
- At 10% of revenue (aggressive, headcount replacement): EUR 3,000-5,000/mo
- **Realistic ceiling: EUR 1,500-3,000/mo for most target agencies**

### Price anchoring

- vs. 1 junior marketing employee (NL): EUR 2,500-3,500/mo loaded cost
- vs. 1 junior marketing employee (UK): GBP 2,500-3,300/mo loaded cost
- vs. GoHighLevel ($97-497/mo): FMai should be 3-5x for 10x the value
- vs. AI agent services (GBP 1,000/mo per agent): FMai bundles multiple agents

### Recommended pricing sweet spot

**EUR 500-1,500/mo** for the core platform, with usage-based upside:
- Starter: EUR 500/mo (5 clients, basic AI agents)
- Growth: EUR 1,000/mo (15 clients, full AI suite)
- Scale: EUR 2,000/mo (30+ clients, white-label, priority)
- Enterprise: EUR 3,000+/mo (unlimited, custom, dedicated support)

**Rationale:** At EUR 1,000/mo, you're less than half the cost of one junior employee, well within the tool budget of a 10-person agency, and positioned as a productivity multiplier rather than just another SaaS tool.
