# UK AI Marketing Platforms - Competitive Research

Research date: 2026-03-25. Focused on companies selling AI marketing tools/agents/platforms to marketing agencies in the UK market, with emphasis on premium/enterprise positioning.

---

## TIER 1: UK-HEADQUARTERED / UK-FOCUSED AI MARKETING PLATFORMS

### 1. Jacquard (formerly Phrasee)
- **URL:** https://www.jacquard.com/
- **HQ:** London, UK
- **What they sell:** AI-powered brand messaging platform. Generates and optimizes marketing copy for email, SMS, and push notifications at scale. "The agentic enterprise platform that creates content that converts."
- **Pricing:** Custom/enterprise only. No public pricing. Capterra lists "Contact vendor." Software Finder says "custom, tailored to each business's needs." G2 lists pricing but requires login. Historical reference: Phrasee starting at $1.00 (likely per unit/message).
- **Target market:** Large enterprise brands (Adidas, United Airlines, Costco, Accor, Hims & Hers)
- **Positioning:** Premium enterprise. Very high-end. Not accessible to SMEs.
- **Unified agent or separate tools?** Single platform focused on messaging optimization. Not a unified marketing agent.
- **Agency-specific plans?** No visible agency program. Targets brands directly.
- **Funding:** Raised $46M+ total. Series B in 2021. Rebranded from Phrasee to Jacquard in 2024.
- **Key insight:** Pure enterprise play. Narrow focus (CRM messaging only). No self-serve.

### 2. Albert.ai (by Zoomd)
- **URL:** https://albert.ai/
- **HQ:** Originally Tel Aviv, but operates heavily in UK/US markets. Parent company Zoomd is publicly traded.
- **What they sell:** Cross-channel autonomous AI platform for digital advertising. Manages paid media campaigns autonomously across Google, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, etc.
- **Pricing:** Custom only. "Contact us for a customized pricing plan." Pricing form asks: Advertiser/brand, Agency, or Consultancy. Budget tiers: $100k-$500k, $500k-$2.5M, $2.5M-$10M, Over $10M yearly ad spend.
- **Target market:** Mid-to-large advertisers and agencies with significant ad spend ($100k+ yearly minimum).
- **Positioning:** Premium enterprise. Positioned as "autonomous AI" that runs campaigns, not just assists.
- **Unified agent or separate tools?** Unified autonomous platform for paid media only.
- **Agency-specific plans?** Yes - agencies and consultancies are a listed customer type on their pricing form.
- **Key insight:** Requires substantial ad budgets. True autonomous execution (not copilot). Narrow focus on paid media.

## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. Market Gaps in UK
- **No dominant UK-based "AI Marketing Employee" platform.** Zaturn (Gibraltar) is closest but positioned as budget.
- **Most UK companies are agencies using AI**, not product companies selling AI platforms.
- **The AaaS framing is emerging** (Worktual uses it explicitly) but no UK company has productized it specifically for marketing agencies.
- **White-label gap:** Stammer is US-based; no UK-native white-label AI marketing platform exists.

### 2. Pricing Patterns
- **Budget self-serve:** $14-69/month (Pencil, Blaze, Zaturn) -- content/social tools
- **Mid-market per-agent:** $99-600/month per agent (MarkeTeam) -- named AI employees
- **Agency white-label:** $197-497/month platform fee + usage (Stammer)
- **Enterprise custom:** $100k+/year (Albert, Jacquard, Persado) -- large brands only
- **Done-for-you hybrid:** GBP 499-2,500/month (UK agencies with AI)

### 3. Positioning Landscape
- **Premium enterprise (narrow):** Jacquard, Persado, Albert -- focus on one channel, custom pricing, Fortune 500 clients
- **Premium enterprise (broad):** Salesforce Agentforce, HubSpot Breeze -- marketing suite with AI bolted on
- **Mid-market unified agent:** Jasper, MarkeTeam -- multiple AI agents, team metaphor
- **Budget unified agent:** Zaturn, Blaze -- accessible pricing, execution-focused
- **Agency enablement:** Stammer, Lety, Plai -- white-label platforms for reselling
- **UK local agencies with AI:** Digital Forest, AI Digital Marketing, Bird -- service companies

### 4. Trends
- **"AI Employee" naming is mainstream:** Zaturn ("AI Marketing Team"), MarkeTeam ("Co-Workers Not Co-Pilots"), Jobix ("AI Employee"), The Agent Bureau ("Digital Staffing")
- **Named agents are common:** Zaturn (Chloe, Emma, Alex), MarkeTeam (Ella, Jane, Daniel, Maya)
- **Execution over advice:** The market is shifting from "AI that suggests" to "AI that executes" -- this is the dominant pitch in 2026
- **Agency white-label demand:** Multiple platforms now offer white-label. Stammer reports $300-500 MRR per client for its agencies.
- **Hybrid human + AI models appearing:** Blaze offers "done for you" tiers ($999-5,999/month) with human strategists plus AI execution

### 5. UK-Specific Considerations
- **GDPR/data residency is a key differentiator.** UK companies emphasize EU/UK data hosting.
- **GBP pricing matters.** UK SMEs prefer pound-denominated plans.
- **UK AI VC is booming:** Over GBP 6 billion raised by UK AI startups in 2025 (80% increase from 2024). 33%+ of all UK VC goes to AI.
- **Government support:** AI Opportunities Action Plan, Sovereign AI Unit (GBP 500M), AI Growth Zones.

### 6. Competitive White Space for FMai
- **No UK-native AaaS platform for marketing agencies** with premium positioning exists.
- **MarkeTeam is closest competitor** conceptually (named AI agents, per-agent pricing) but is Israel-based and expensive ($600/agent/month).
- **Stammer dominates agency white-label** but is chat/voice only, not marketing execution.
- **Zaturn is the budget alternative** but lacks premium positioning and agency depth.
- **Opportunity:** UK-based, premium-positioned, unified AI marketing agent platform specifically for agencies, with GBP pricing, GDPR compliance, and proper white-label capabilities.
